Best Destinations to Visit In India



Lake Pichola, situated in Udaipur city in the Indian state of Rajasthan, is an artificial fresh water lake, created in the year 1362 AD, named after the nearby Picholi village. It is one of the several contiguous lakes, and developed over the last few centuries in and around the famous Udaipur city.

Architectural style: Indo-European architecture

Design life: Created in the year 1362 AD, named after the nearby Picholi village.

About 95 Kms from Shillong-The capital of Meghalaya and just a mile close to Indo-Bangladesh International border in the west Jaintia Hills, the town named Dawki is famous for its pristine green surroundings and of course the Umngot River which i would say is among the cleanest river in whole of India. Boating over the very transparent water of the river where you can actually see the river bed is the major attraction of the place. Apart from this, the town is also developed as a trading town of the two countries i.e. India and Bangladesh.

The River is about 15-20 feet in depth during winter

The river is colorless and see-through

A living root bridge is a type of simple suspension bridge formed of living plant roots by tree shaping. They are handmade from the aerial roots of rubber fig trees (Ficus elastica) by the Khasi and Jaintia peoples of the mountainous terrain along the southern part of the Shillong Plateau.

Material: Living trees roots

Design life: up to 500 years

Total length: over 50 meters

Width: over 1.5 meters
